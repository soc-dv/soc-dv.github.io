    About Us

    SoC-DV is a research group specializing in FPGA, ASIC design, SoCs, Embedded Systems, and more. Our team has extensive experience in delivering innovative solutions in verification, design, and development of complex hardware systems.

    Objectives

    The primary objective of our SoC development research group is to drive innovation in SoC design and integration, aiming to deliver cutting-edge solutions that address emerging technology needs. We strive to set new industry standards through groundbreaking research and practical implementations.

    Core Focus Areas

    Our core focus areas include developing advanced design techniques to enhance SoC performance and efficiency, fostering interdisciplinary collaboration to leverage diverse expertise, and creating and validating prototypes to ensure high functionality and performance.

    Strategic Initiatives

    To achieve these goals, our strategic initiatives prioritize research into new technologies and design practices for next-generation SoCs, enhance design toolchains and methodologies for more efficient development, and build strong industry partnerships while focusing on continuous talent growth.

    PhD Students

    Engr. Memoona Gul

    Lecturer, Electronic Engineering Department, UET Peshawar, Abbottabad Campus

    Engr. Memoona Gul is a dedicated PhD student and passionate member of the SoC development group at UET Peshawar, Abbottabad Campus. Her research focuses on the Acceleration of Power-Efficient Transformer Neural Network using FPGA, where she aims to enhance Transformer models' performance and energy efficiency using FPGA technology. With a strong background in FPGA based system design, she also completed her MS research on the Effect of Antenna Type and Body Mass Index on Characteristics of On-body Channels, contributing valuable insights to wireless body area networks. Her expertise and drive make her a key figure in advancing FPGA and AI innovation.

    Publications:
    1. An Advanced DSP Algorithm for Music-Less Audio Stream Generation.
    2. Generalized method for Harmonic Elimination in Two and Three Level Voltage Sourced Converters.
